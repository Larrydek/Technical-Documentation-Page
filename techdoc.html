Introduction

Life is beautiful and complex, but can be studied as another science phenomenom. In this case life is based on small living things called cells, but this cells are made of smaller biological systems that are made of molecules that are made of atoms. However, this is not a full course, but we will try to review the conceps and principles that made life possible, and to see the general vision of the repeating patterns that generates this complex cycle called life

What you should already know

Before to continue this review, you have to take a look on atomic structure. The proton is the particle that has positive charge and lie in the nucleus of the atom between the neutron (attached by the strong nuclear force, which is significantly stronger that electromagnetic force) electron is the particle of the atom that has negative charge.

You always have to take present the concept of electronegativity. This phenomenom is basically the desire that an atom has to win or lose an electron. This desire depends on the atomic number (Z = number of protons in the nucleus) and the atomic radius of the element You could think that bonds between atoms are the action of electronegativity taking place when 2 or more atoms meet in space.

Let's suppose there are 2 atoms close to each other of the corresponding elements A and B.

  • If electronegativity of A ≈ B, and are big ones (e.g. 2 non metals) there will be a COVALENT BOND, because both atoms want the other's electron.
  • If electronegativity of A ≈ B, and are little one (e.g. 2 metals) there will be a METAL BOND, because they want to donate its electron to the other atom. This usually forms a cristaline net of electrons and this is the cause of electric conductivity on metals when subjected to a potential difference.
  • If electronegativity of A ≠ B, and A,B ≠ 0
